## 2. v3.5 mission

> Ship a fast, replayable movie-linking game that makes the player want One more go and makes a completed chain worth sharing.

v3.5 is a meaningful product milestone. It brings repeated gameplay, Fame, Runs, progression, Instant ReelPlay and challenge sharing together as one coherent experience.

The 48-hour mission is not to prove the complete long-term vision. It is to put the strongest coherent version in front of real people and learn from what they actually do.

> Real players create the baseline. The baseline creates the targets.

## 3. What Reel Chain is

Reel Chain is a movie connection game. A player is given a starting movie and a destination movie, then finds the movies and actors that connect them.

> Connect two movies through the actors who appeared in them.

The pleasure comes from recognising a connection, finding a route, completing the chain, watching it rebuild through Instant ReelPlay, building Fame and deciding to have One more go.

## 4. Audience

The initial audience is people who enjoy films, trivia, connections and quick puzzle games. Reel Chain should reward strong film knowledge without requiring the player to be a film obsessive.

Launch positioning can become more precise after v3.5 produces real behavioural and qualitative feedback. It must not delay the build.

## 5. The number one objective: One more go

> The number one objective of Reel Chain is to make the player want One more go.

One more go is both the primary player-facing call to action and the internal test for product decisions.

Every gameplay, design and technical decision should be judged by one question:

> Does this make the player more or less likely to have One more go?

The core loop is:

> Start a chain → solve it → earn Fame → watch Instant ReelPlay → One more go or challenge a friend

Players can start as many rounds as they want. The next round should begin quickly, without a menu detour, waiting state or artificial restriction.

## 6. Measures of success

v3.5 will establish a real-world behavioural baseline before numerical targets are imposed.

### Primary gameplay measure

> One More Go rate: the percentage of players who start another round after completing one.

Supporting measures: rounds started, rounds completed, completion rate, average rounds per Run, percentage reaching a third round, time from completion to the next round and the point at which players leave.

### Primary growth measure

> New players generated by shared Instant ReelPlays and challenge links.

Supporting measures: share actions, successful GIF generation, challenge-link visits, challenge starts, challenge completions and further shares generated by challenge recipients.

## 7. Core gameplay

The existing playable mechanic should be preserved and improved rather than replaced during the 48-hour build.

1. The player sees a starting movie and a destination movie.
2. The player chooses an actor who appeared in the current movie.
3. The player chooses another movie featuring that actor.
4. The process continues until the destination movie is reached.
5. The round is scored using the approved Fame model.
6. The completed chain is rebuilt through Instant ReelPlay.
7. The player chooses One more go or shares a challenge.

There is no timer-based pressure, hard attempt limit or forced loss after a number of mistakes. The player continues until they solve the chain or choose Give Up.

## 8. What counts as a link

One link is one movie placed between the previous movie and the next movie.

> Previous movie → Actor 1 → Link movie → Actor 2 → Next movie

Actor 1 appeared in the previous movie and the link movie. Actor 2 appeared in the link movie and the next movie.

The starting and destination movies are the two ends of the Reel Chain. The movies found between them are the links.

Example:

> Movie A → Actor 1 → Movie B → Actor 2 → Movie C

Movie B is one link. This definition must be used consistently in scoring, analytics, completion copy, challenge targets and sharing.

## 9. Fame scoring

Reel Chain uses Fame as its points system. Harder chains award more Fame. The round score rewards efficient solving and deducts Fame for unnecessary moves, wrong turns and help.

| Difficulty | Base Fame |
| --- | --- |
| Easy | 30 |
| Medium | 50 |
| Hard | 80 |

| Round event | Fame effect |
| --- | --- |
| Each additional link beyond the shortest known chain | −5 Fame |
| Each failed connection, discarded choice, undo or backtrack | −2 Fame |
| Each hint used | −5 Fame |
| Time taken | No effect |
| Completed-round minimum | At least 1 Fame |

**Round Fame = base difficulty Fame − extra links − wrong turns/backtracks − hints**

Difficulty labels may remain internal. The player does not need to see Easy, Medium or Hard unless that presentation improves the experience.

> v3.5 clarification (2026-07-11): **Surprise difficulty is removed.** All generated chains are classified as Easy, Medium or Hard only.

The scoring engine should use the shortest known valid chain as its efficiency reference. Hermes must confirm that the current data and route logic can calculate this reliably, but should not redesign the agreed scoring model.

## 10. Runs and milestone bonuses

A Run is a sequence of completed rounds. Run rewards are separate from the Fame score for the individual chain just completed.

| Run milestone | Bonus Fame |
| --- | --- |
| 3 completed rounds | +5 Fame |
| 6 completed rounds | +10 Fame |
| 9 completed rounds | +15 Fame |

The milestone bonus must be shown as an additional Run reward, not folded invisibly into the score for the last chain.

A Run continues while the player keeps solving chains. One more go advances the player to the next round in the same Run.

## 11. Give Up

Give Up is the single surrender action. There is no separate Reveal button.

Runs use a **banked-milestone** model (v3.5 decision, ratified 2026-07-11):

- Completing **Round 3**, **Round 6** and **Round 9** banks the player's current lifetime Fame.
- Between milestones, every point of Fame earned is **at risk**.
- When the player chooses Give Up, they forfeit all unbanked (at-risk) Fame and begin a new Run starting from the banked total. Established Hall of Fame history is kept — Give Up never wipes banked progress.

This replaces the earlier "fixed Fame penalty / return to round one" wording. There is no single fixed penalty; the risk is exactly the Fame earned since the last banked milestone, shown live in the Run UI ("Banked: X · At risk: Y") and again on the Give Up confirm step so the player always knows what they are sacrificing.

The shortest known chain is still revealed on Give Up, and a new Run begins from the banked total.

## 12. Progression and Hall of Fame

Progression is required in v3.5. Repeated rounds must visibly build the player’s personal movie status.

The first build should include the simplest coherent version of:

- Cumulative Fame.
- Current Hall of Fame status or level.
- Visible progress towards the next status.
- Persistence when the player refreshes or returns on the same device.

The complete future Hall of Fame, actor avatars, detailed achievements, accounts and cross-device profiles should not delay v3.5.

Hermes should preserve any existing v3 status tiers and thresholds where they already work. Presentation can be improved through live review rather than prolonged abstract debate.

## 13. Completion experience

Completing a chain should feel rewarding, clear and immediately connected to the next action.

The completion hierarchy is:

1. Chain Complete.
2. Instant ReelPlay.
3. Fame earned and Run milestone reward, where applicable.
4. Visible Hall of Fame progress.
5. One more go.
6. Challenge a friend / Share Instant ReelPlay.

One more go is the primary action. Sharing is visible and compelling, but secondary to continuing play.

The completion state must not become a dense results dashboard. Do not add panels for time, steps, run streaks or other statistics that compete with the chain.

## 14. Instant ReelPlay

Instant ReelPlay is the external, brandable name of Reel Chain’s hero share asset.

When a round is completed, Reel Chain must automatically play an eye-catching in-page animation that reconstructs the completed chain using the actual movie posters and actor headshots from that round.

Instant ReelPlay is both:

- The hero moment of the in-game completion experience.
- The social asset the player should immediately want to share.

> The player watches their Instant ReelPlay and thinks: I want other people to see that.

### Required sequence

1. Reveal the starting movie.
2. Reveal the connecting actor.
3. Reveal the next movie.
4. Continue through each actor and link movie.
5. Reveal the destination movie.
6. Resolve into a strong branded final frame with the link count and a challenge invitation.

### Design requirements

- Vertically aligned movies, with actor headshots clearly connecting them.
- Support for different chain lengths without redesigning the composition.
- Legibility on a phone and inside a social feed.
- Clear visual explanation of the movie-to-actor-to-movie relationship, even for someone who has never played.
- Eye-catching pacing, transitions and emphasis that feel deliberately designed rather than technically animated.
- A clean end frame that can stand alone outside the game.
- Neutral completion language such as Chain Complete.
- No claim that the chain is uniquely best or unbeatable.

The chain must not be presented as a horizontal timeline. The composition should feel capable of extending as links are added, not like a fixed journey towards a visible endpoint.

Hermes owns the functional experience, data sequence, assets, variable-length layout, rendering and integration. A design or motion specialist should be involved during the first build when needed to meet the creative standard; specialist input is not a reason to defer the feature.

## 15. Animated Instant ReelPlay GIF

The animated share GIF is part of the first v3.5 build round. It is not a later enhancement.

When the in-page Instant ReelPlay completes, Reel Chain must generate a shareable animated GIF from the real completed round.

The GIF must:

- Use the actual posters, headshots and chain sequence.
- Match the visual language of the in-page Instant ReelPlay.
- Support variable chain lengths.
- Remain readable in a mobile social feed.
- Include Reel Chain branding, the start and destination movies, the link count and a simple Can you beat it? invitation.
- Finish on a strong final frame.
- Exclude dense statistics, interface chrome and unsupported claims.

The share action should attach the GIF where the device and platform allow, and include the challenge URL in the accompanying share text. Provide a practical fallback that lets the player save the GIF and copy the challenge link.

The first build round is not complete unless both the in-page Instant ReelPlay and the animated share GIF work from a real completed chain.

## 16. Challenge experience

The primary challenge experience preserves the same starting movie and destination movie. The recipient tries to complete the chain in fewer links than the original player.

The challenge link must preserve:

- The starting movie.
- The destination movie.
- The original player’s link count.
- Attribution that the visit came from a challenge.

The recipient is challenging the result, not copying the original route. The original route should not be shown before the recipient plays.

The challenge landing experience should make the task immediately clear and let the recipient begin without unnecessary setup.

### Fallback

When the original puzzle cannot be loaded reliably, Reel Chain should generate a new playable chain rather than show an error or dead end.

The fallback must not falsely claim that the recipient is playing the same movies or beating the original link count.

## 17. Share copy and final frame

The share asset should be simple enough to work when encountered cold in a social feed.

Recommended structure:

> CHAIN COMPLETE
> Andy connected [Start Movie] to [Destination Movie] in [X] links.
> Can you beat it?

The final wording can adapt to whether a player name is available, but should remain brief, confident and neutral.

Do not add completion time, step panels, streak panels, dense Fame breakdowns or multiple competing calls to action.

## 18. v3.5 release priorities

### P0 — First substantive v3.5 build round

P0 must work as one coherent live experience. It includes:

- Stable, unlimited repeat gameplay.
- One more go as the primary repeat-play action.
- The approved Fame scoring model.
- Runs and 3 / 6 / 9 milestone bonuses.
- Give Up costing Fame, ending the Run, revealing the shortest chain and restarting from round one.
- Cumulative Fame and visible Hall of Fame progression.
- The complete in-page Instant ReelPlay.
- Real movie posters and actor headshots.
- A vertical, variable-length chain.
- Eye-catching motion designed to be worth sharing.
- An animated Instant ReelPlay GIF generated from the completed round.
- Immediate sharing of the GIF with a challenge link.
- Neutral Chain Complete language.
- Same-start-and-destination challenge links with the original link count as the target.
- A newly generated playable chain as fallback.
- Analytics covering gameplay, One more go, Give Up, Instant ReelPlay, GIF generation, sharing and challenge acquisition.
- A strong mobile experience.

P0 is not complete merely because each feature technically exists. Gameplay, scoring, progression, completion, animation, sharing and the next action must feel like one product.

### P1 — Next major build series

P1 is the next substantial series of improvements after the v3.5 P0 experience is coherent and live. It currently includes:

- Richer Hall of Fame progression and player-status presentation.
- Challenge-result comparison and clearer acknowledgement of whether the target was beaten.
- Further Instant ReelPlay motion and visual development based on real sharing behaviour.
- Improved GIF generation quality and platform compatibility.
- Deeper gameplay analytics and funnel diagnostics.
- Achievements where evidence shows they support continued play.
- Actor-avatar identity.
- Improvements identified through real-player feedback from v3.5.

Accounts, cross-device progression, leaderboards, friend systems and additional game modes remain later product development unless evidence changes the priority.

The first build must make the two objectives measurable: One more go and organic acquisition through sharing.

| link_added | A link movie is added to the chain. |

| hint_used | A hint is used. |

Use the analytics service already present where possible. Do not introduce a complex platform migration during the 48-hour build.

## 23. Acceptance criteria

v3.5 P0 passes when:

- A new visitor can understand how to begin without a long explanation.
- A player can complete a valid movie-to-movie chain.
- One link is counted as one intermediary movie.
- The player can choose One more go and begin the next round quickly.
- Fame is calculated using the approved model.
- Run milestones are awarded separately at 3, 6 and 9 completed rounds.
- Give Up costs Fame, ends the Run, reveals the shortest chain and restarts from round one.
- Cumulative Fame and Hall of Fame progress persist after refresh on the same device.
- The completed chain is displayed vertically using real posters and actor headshots.
- Different chain lengths do not break the layout.
- Instant ReelPlay automatically reconstructs the completed chain in-page.
- The animation is eye-catching, understandable and intentionally designed.
- An animated GIF is generated from the real completed chain.
- The GIF remains readable in a mobile social feed.
- The share action includes the challenge URL.
- The challenge normally loads the same start and destination movies with the original link count as the target.
- A new playable chain is supplied when the original challenge cannot load.
- Neutral completion language is used.
- One More Go rate and share-to-challenge acquisition can be measured.
- The mobile experience has no blocking clipping, overlap or unreadable text.
